003 : Predicting Heart disease using machine learning

This project focuses on applying machine learning techniques to predict the likelihood of heart disease in patients based on clinical data. Heart disease is one of the leading causes of death globally, and early detection can greatly improve patient outcomes. By leveraging machine learning, this project seeks to build a predictive model that can assist healthcare professionals in identifying at-risk individuals more efficiently. The process involves working with a well-known dataset from the Cleveland Heart Disease study, available through the UCI Machine Learning Repository, and also accessible on Kaggle. This dataset includes various clinical parameters that are commonly measured in medical exams, such as age, cholesterol levels, resting blood pressure, and results from stress tests. Machine learning algorithms (e.g., logistic regression, decision trees, or neural networks) are typically used to train the model. The goal is to train a model that can analyze these factors and predict whether or not a patient is likely to have heart disease.
This project aims to provide a solution for one of the most critical challenges in healthcare: predicting and preventing heart disease through data-driven insights.
